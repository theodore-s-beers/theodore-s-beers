### Hello 👋

I’m a **research software engineer** with a particular love for the digital humanities, and a classically trained Persian and Arabic philologist (PhD UChicago, 2020) with an active [research agenda](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=dqd1ahUAAAAJ).

Since summer 2025, I’ve been working at [Boot.dev](https://www.boot.dev/) as their **Senior Course Producer** – currently the only employee focusing primarily on course content, collaborating closely with the CEO and with outside authors. This company has turned out to be a great environment for someone like me, passionate about both computer programming and pedagogy. I feel extremely fortunate to have found the opportunity.

Prior to that, I spent the 2024–25 academic year working in the Colleges of Engineering and [Computing & Informatics](https://mrc.cci.drexel.edu/) at Drexel University in Philadelphia. There I served as the main software engineer on an NSF-funded project that involved (*inter alia*) setting up and administering clusters of GPU servers to be used by researchers for training machine learning models. Many of my responsibilities fell under the umbrella of what might be called **Research DevOps/MLOps**.

I’m still based in the Philadelphia area, and I think I might stay a while. I hope someday to return to working full-time at a university. That may need to wait, however, for a better climate for research funding (if one ever comes).
